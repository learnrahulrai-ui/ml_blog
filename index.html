The Trade Curve: Sliding the Cutoff and What AUC Actually Measures
2026-06-05 · part 2 of 4 · roc-curve, auc, cutoff
One cutoff judges the machine at one spot; slide it through every position and you get the ROC (trade) curve. The area beneath it — AUC — compresses the whole curve to one number with a clean probabilistic reading: pick a random sick lump and a random well lump; AUC is the chance the machine scored the sick one higher.

Leash and Cloud: L2 Punishment and the Two-Cloud Wall
2026-06-05 · part 3 of 4 · l2-regularization, lda
Two ways to humble an overconfident machine. L2: add a squared-dial price to the loss so no single dial dominates (C = 1/λ — small C is a heavy squeeze, not light). LDA: forget rolling downhill entirely — read the two cloud centres and their shared spread, then solve for the wall in one closed-form step. Both machines find the same boundary; the agreement proves the data is cleanly separable.
